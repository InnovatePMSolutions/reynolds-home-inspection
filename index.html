Premium Ancillary Testing

These high-value add-on services protect you from the hidden hazards and underground liabilities that a standard inspection cannot see. Every one is available as a standalone or bundled with your core inspection for maximum efficiency and savings.

Radon Gas Testing

Radon is invisible, odorless, tasteless, and the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States. It seeps naturally from the soil beneath a property and accumulates to dangerous levels indoors without any warning signs whatsoever. Sellers rarely conduct radon testing on their own initiative. For every buyer, regardless of home age or location, radon testing is strongly recommended by the EPA, the Illinois Emergency Management Agency, and every major inspection trade organization. We conduct certified radon testing and provide results and mitigation recommendations as part of your complete report.

Add to Your Inspection

Mold & Indoor Air Quality (IAQ) Sampling

Hidden moisture problems and elevated mold spore counts don't show up on a standard visual inspection — they hide behind drywall, inside attic cavities, beneath flooring, and in HVAC systems. By the time mold becomes visible, it frequently signals a larger, more costly moisture intrusion event already underway. Our certified mold and indoor air quality sampling identifies airborne particulate concentrations, pinpoints suspect moisture zones, and gives you the documentation you need to negotiate repairs before closing — or walk away from a property that poses a serious structural and health risk. Identifying these problems during your contingency window can save you tens of thousands of dollars in remediation costs after the fact.

Add to Your Inspection

Sewer Line Video Scope Inspection

The sewer lateral line running from the home to the municipal connection is one of the single most expensive systems a property owner can face replacing — and it is entirely underground and completely invisible to a standard inspection. Tree root intrusion, pipe corrosion, offset joints, bellied sections, and collapsed lines are common in homes across the Chicago metro area, particularly in older neighborhoods where cast iron and clay tile pipes have been in the ground for 50 to 100 years. A camera-equipped sewer scope inspection looks directly inside the line, capturing real-time video evidence of the pipe's current condition. Catching a compromised sewer line before closing can save you between $5,000 and $25,000 or more in emergency excavation and replacement costs. This is one of the highest-value add-ons available for any buyer, and we recommend it for virtually every property inspection we perform.
